The RACE Framework

🎯 Question: Who can explain how the RACE framework works?

R · Role
Who should the A.I. pretend to be? (e.g. game developer, sound designer)
A · Action
What do you want the A.I. to DO? (e.g. add a sound, change colours)
C · Context
What background info does the A.I. need to know? (e.g. browser Tetris, React)
E · Expectation
What should the result look or sound like? (e.g. retro 8-bit chime, 0.3s)
I Do

The RACE Framework

Every time you prompt the A.I., think about four things:

R Role

Who should the A.I. be?

A game developer, a sound designer, a colour expert...

A Action

What do you want it to DO?

Change colours, add a sound, build a feature...

C Context

What does the A.I. need to know?

This is a Tetris game in Canvas mode using React...

E Expectation

What should the result look like?

A short retro beep, distinct colour shades...

R — A — C — E = Better prompts, better results!

What's a Superpower Feature?

A superpower is a NEW feature you add to your Tetris game using a RACE prompt. Something the original didn't have. Here's the example I'll walk you through:

★★★ Super Power · Bomb Block You are a game developer. Add a bomb block that appears every time 5 rows are cleared. This is a React-based Tetris game with score tracking. The bomb should be a gold colour, clear a 3×3 area when it lands, and trigger a short explosion animation with a "boom" sound.

Spot the RACE pieces

  • R: Game developer
  • A: Add a bomb block after 5 rows
  • C: React Tetris with score tracking
  • E: Gold bomb, 3×3 clear, explosion + sound

Evaluate Your Output

Check your work

  • Have you sent your RACE prompt?
  • Does the change match your Expectation?
  • Did you get the result you wanted?
  • If something is wrong, which part of RACE will your next prompt improve?

If it didn't work...

Write another RACE prompt. This time, be more specific in your Expectation or Context.

Debug Tip

Colours wrong? Make your Expectation clearer.
Sound too loud? Adjust your Context or Expectation.
